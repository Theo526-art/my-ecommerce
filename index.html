HOW TO STEEP TEA LIKE A PRO

Steeping tea is a simple but mindful process that brings out its best flavor. Start by heating fresh, cold water to the right temperature—boiling for black tea, slightly cooler for green or white teas. Place your tea leaves or tea bag in a clean cup or teapot. Pour the hot water gently over the tea, allowing it to release its natural aroma. Let it steep for the recommended time, usually 2–5 minutes depending on the type. Avoid over-steeping, as this can make the tea taste bitter. Finally, remove the leaves or bag, and enjoy your warm, soothing cup.

All ABOUT TEA AROMAS

Tea aromas are a delightful part of the drinking experience, ranging from floral and fruity to earthy and smoky. The scent often gives a preview of the flavor, awakening the senses before the first sip. Each type of tea carries its own signature fragrance, shaped by the leaves and how they’re processed. Breathing in the aroma creates a moment of calm, making tea more than just a drink—it’s an experience.
